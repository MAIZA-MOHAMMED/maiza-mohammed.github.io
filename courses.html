Teaching Practices

I teach a wide range of computer science topics to diverse audiences in both bachelor's and master's programs. As a course coordinator, I am responsible for delivering lectures, tutorials, and practical work for the modules listed above. I design the pedagogical materials, including course slides, exercise sheets, practical lab manuals, and assessment materials, which are distributed online via the university's learning management platform.

In my teaching, I emphasize hands-on learning through practical coding exercises and projects. For programming courses, I use project-based learning approaches where students build real-world applications to reinforce theoretical concepts. I also incorporate collaborative learning strategies, peer programming exercises, and pair work to enhance student engagement and knowledge sharing.

I regularly supervise end-of-year projects and internships for both Bachelor's and Master's students, guiding them through the research process and helping them develop their technical and professional skills.

Student Assessment and Support

I use a combination of formative and summative assessments to evaluate student progress, including written exams, practical lab tests, project presentations, and continuous assessment. I provide regular feedback to students and offer office hours for individual consultation and support.

Continuous Improvement

I actively participate in pedagogical training sessions and workshops offered by the university to stay updated on innovative teaching methodologies. I continually adapt my teaching materials and approaches based on student feedback and learning outcomes to ensure the highest quality of education.

  • Training in project-based learning and active pedagogies
  • Workshops on digital tools for education (Moodle, virtual classrooms)
  • Participation in experience-sharing meetings on teaching innovations
